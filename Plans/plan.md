Where we are: Physics v1 is structurally done — the sim loop now runs on { frame, world } through contact, constraint slab, and wall resolution, with worldProps as the canonical prop list and runKineticPhysics(tick, dt, hooks) as the orchestration boundary (game hooks assembled in the engine; snake registers combat via its session). What’s left from the peel roadmap is Part 4 only: a GridNavContext for nav/perception so the grid isn’t re-hit lazily on every vision step and worker payload. On the feature tree, v1 is maintenance-ready; the headline gaps are still Trilogy C (persistent manifolds, revolute/motor joints, breakable links) and chain-vs-wall sweep (v1 accepts tail clip in narrow corridors).

Where to go next: GridNavContext is the clean standalone slice — it completes the “peel at boundaries” story for nav without touching physics again. After that, snake is the best proving ground for the next physics bit: it already stress-tests distance constraints, link-capsule wall projection (linkCapsuleWall.test.js), multi-head combat splits (snakeMinLengthDeath), glass fracture on contact, and a perf budget with 30 agents (snakePerfBudget). Running predator–prey combat, corridor growth, and break-on-impact link rules through the peeled stack gives you real gameplay signal before investing in revolute joints or CCD — and every failure mode (wedged chains, split topology bumps, contact side effects) maps directly to tests you already have or are one scenario away from adding.
