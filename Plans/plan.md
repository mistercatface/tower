Part 1 — Clean Assets & Physical Joint Eligibility
We will remove the temporary \_chainLinkEligible and extrudeHeight shims by introducing a dedicated flee_wedge asset in Assets/props/ that defines its own correct default physical footprint, rendering height, and tags. Instead of hardcoding "nav" tags or checking instance-level overrides in isChainLinkBall, we will transition chainLinks.js to check a generic canChain flag defined directly in the asset strategy definitions. This preserves the asset catalog as the single source of truth for physical geometry and rendering recipes, ensuring that scaled wedges serialize correctly in snapshots and generate valid, collision-accurate keys in the sprite cache upon reload.

Part 2 — Unified Agent Population Manager
We will refactor the snake-specific and flee-agent-specific registries into a single, unified AgentPopulationManager hosted on the sandbox state. All active entities—whether they are snakes, flee agents, or future species—will register with a common interface tracking their anchor ID, species identifier, faction metadata, and active lifecycle state. The game orchestrator in setupSnakeGame.js will loop through this single manager to tick, sync, and validate agents, making the top-level loop completely agnostic to species and eliminating duplicated tracking structures.

Part 3 — Species & Faction Hostility Matrix
We will replace the hardcoded size-based snake-vs-snake checks in snakeIntent.js with a generic spatial perception query and a Species/Faction Hostility Matrix. When an agent scans its vision cone, it will query the spatial frame for nearby active entities and resolve their relationship via a central helper, such as getRelationship(agentA, agentB), which returns "threat", "prey", "ally", or "neutral". This allows both snakes and flee agents to share the exact same spatial memory, target-memory, and perception pipelines, while letting snakes dynamically classify smaller snakes and flee agents as "prey", and flee agents classify all snakes as "threat".

Part 4 — Decoupled Locomotion & Brain Orchestrator
We will decouple the game loop from the snake-specific FSM by introducing a generic agent tick interface where the orchestrator simply calls instance.tick(dtMs) on all active instances. We will extract common agent behaviors—such as updating spatial cell memory, HPA pathfinding, and throttling off-screen perception updates—into a shared AgentBrain or AgentLocomotion helper. SnakeAutosim and FleeAgentAutosim will then compose this shared helper, keeping the core navigation and memory mechanics in the engine layer while leaving specific FSM state transitions to the domain-specific intent adapters.

Part 5 — Unified Combat & Collision System
We will replace the four separate snake-specific contact resolvers with a single, unified CombatSystem that maps any physical segment ID to its owning AgentInstance. When a contact is detected between two bodies, the system will look up the owning agents, check their relationship via the hostility matrix, and—if a predator-prey relationship exists above a velocity threshold—dispatch a generic damage or kill event to the victim. The victim agent will handle its own specific death sequence, allowing snakes to split or shatter and flee agents to unregister and fracture into edible shards, while automatically transitioning any targeting predators from seek_prey to seek_food.
