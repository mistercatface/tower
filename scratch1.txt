1. Optimize EntityRegistry to Avoid $O(\text{Total Entities})$ Scans
Currently, the

EntityRegistry
 class stores all registered entities in a single map: this._entries = new Map().

The Issue: Functions like forEachOfKind(kind, fn) loop through the entire map of entities:
javascript
forEachOfKind(kind, fn) {
    for (const entry of this._entries.values()) if (entry.kind === kind) fn(entry.ref);
}
This turns every scan for a specific kind into a full map traversal over every registered entity (projectiles, corpses, floating texts, etc.). Because forEachOfKind("worldProp") is called frequently in hot paths (physics passes, rendering, auto-combat, etc.), this creates substantial CPU overhead as the entity count grows.
The Recommendation: Group entities by their kind in the registry upon registration (e.g. keeping a Map<string, Set<object>> or Map<string, Array<object>> in this._byKind). Update register and unregister to add/remove references from their corresponding category collections. This turns forEachOfKind into a simple, direct traversal of the specific sub-collection, shifting operations to $O(\text{Entities of Kind})$.

The Issue: If a prop is static or asleep (which is often true for decorations or static obstacles), running the update loop over the entire worldProps array for every physics substep wastes CPU cycles.
